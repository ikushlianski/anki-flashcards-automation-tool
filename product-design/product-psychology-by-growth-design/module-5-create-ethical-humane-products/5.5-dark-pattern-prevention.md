# 5.5 Dark Pattern Prevention

## Core Concept
Identifying and avoiding dark patterns that manipulate users.

## Key Learning Points
- **Dark Pattern Recognition**: Identifying manipulative design patterns
- **Prevention Strategies**: Avoiding dark patterns in design
- **Alternative Approaches**: Finding ethical alternatives to dark patterns
- **User Education**: Helping users recognize and avoid dark patterns

## Common Dark Patterns
- **Bait and Switch**: Promising one thing but delivering another
- **Hidden Costs**: Hiding additional costs until checkout
- **Forced Continuity**: Making it difficult to cancel subscriptions
- **Confirmshaming**: Guilt-tripping users into making choices
- **Roach Motel**: Making it easy to get in but hard to get out

## Practical Applications
- **Pattern Identification**: Recognizing dark patterns in existing products
- **Design Review**: Reviewing designs to prevent dark patterns
- **Team Education**: Teaching teams about dark patterns
- **Policy Creation**: Creating policies against dark patterns

## Exercises
1. **Pattern Audit**: Audit your product for dark patterns
2. **Alternative Design**: Create ethical alternatives to dark patterns
3. **Team Training**: Train your team to recognize and avoid dark patterns

## Key Takeaways
- Dark patterns erode user trust and damage long-term relationships
- Ethical alternatives often perform better than manipulative patterns
- Team education prevents accidental dark pattern implementation
- Clear policies help maintain ethical design standards
