# 6.2 Retention & Churn Prevention

## Core Concept
Building habit-forming experiences that keep users engaged long-term and prevent churn.

## Key Learning Points
- **Habit Loop Psychology**: Trigger → Action → Reward → Investment
- **Engagement Psychology**: Understanding what drives continued usage
- **Churn Prediction**: Identifying users at risk of leaving
- **Reactivation Strategies**: Bringing back dormant users
- **Value Reinforcement**: Continuously demonstrating product value

## SaaS Retention Psychology
- **Usage Patterns**: Creating natural usage rhythms
- **Feature Stickiness**: Making features indispensable
- **Social Engagement**: Building community and peer connections
- **Progress Tracking**: Showing users their growth and achievements
- **Personal Investment**: Getting users to invest time/data in the product

## Practical Applications
- **Habit Formation Design**: Creating experiences that become automatic
- **Engagement Loop Design**: Building self-reinforcing usage patterns
- **Churn Prevention Systems**: Identifying and addressing at-risk users
- **Reactivation Campaigns**: Bringing back dormant users
- **Retention Analytics**: Measuring psychological retention indicators

## Exercises
1. **Habit Loop Design**: Create habit-forming experiences for your product
2. **Churn Risk Analysis**: Identify psychological indicators of churn risk
3. **Engagement Strategy**: Design ongoing engagement experiences
4. **Reactivation Plan**: Create strategies to win back dormant users

## Key Takeaways
- Retention is more important than acquisition for SaaS success
- Habits are formed through consistent trigger-action-reward loops
- Users churn when they don't see ongoing value
- Social engagement significantly improves retention
- Personal investment increases switching costs
