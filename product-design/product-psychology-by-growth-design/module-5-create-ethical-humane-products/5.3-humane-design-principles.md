# 5.3 Humane Design Principles

## Core Concept
The three fundamental principles to follow when designing for a more humane experience.

## The 3 Fundamental Principles
1. **Respect User Agency**: Give users control over their experience
2. **Minimize Harm**: Avoid causing psychological or physical harm
3. **Maximize Benefit**: Ensure products provide genuine value to users

## Key Learning Points
- **Agency Preservation**: Maintaining user control and choice
- **Harm Prevention**: Avoiding negative psychological impacts
- **Value Creation**: Ensuring genuine user benefit
- **Long-term Thinking**: Considering long-term user well-being

## Practical Applications
- **Design Guidelines**: Creating humane design guidelines
- **Decision Making**: Making design decisions that respect users
- **Team Education**: Teaching teams about humane design
- **Product Development**: Integrating humane principles into development

## Exercises
1. **Principle Application**: Apply humane principles to your product
2. **Guideline Creation**: Create humane design guidelines for your team
3. **Decision Evaluation**: Evaluate design decisions against humane principles

## Key Takeaways
- User agency is fundamental to humane design
- Harm prevention is more important than feature addition
- Genuine value creation builds long-term user relationships
- Long-term thinking prevents short-term optimization that harms users
